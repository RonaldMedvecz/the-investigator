**Incident Report: Ransomware Attack**
=====================================

### Summary

A ransomware attack was detected on May 2nd, 2026. The attack involved initial access through a compromised login credential, followed by lateral movement and encryption of critical files. The attackers attempted to extort payment in exchange for decryption keys.

### Timeline

* 01:14:07 - Failed login attempt from 185.220.101.47
* 01:20:55 - Successful login from same IP address
* 01:22:03 - Creation of a new file on the file server
* 01:31:40-42 - Multiple files were encrypted and renamed with '.locked'
* 09:00:05-47 - Network traffic indicating communication with external IP addresses

### Root Cause

The initial access was through a compromised login credential. The attackers likely used phishing or another social engineering attack to gain unauthorized access.

### Runbook Compliance

The following runbook steps were completed:

* Preparation: 1/4
	+ Identification of critical assets and data was not performed.
	+ Offline or immutable backups were not maintained, although they were available.
* Detection & Analysis: 2/6
	+ Detection indicators (mass file renames, failed-then-successful logins) were detected.
	+ Evidence was preserved before remediation.
* Containment, Eradication & Recovery: 3/5
	+ Isolation of infected hosts on the network was performed.
	+ Malware and ransom payloads were removed from contained systems.

The following runbook steps were missed:

* Preparation: 1/4 (identification of critical assets and data)
* Detection & Analysis: 5/6 (no review of logs to determine initial access)
* Containment, Eradication & Recovery: 2/5 (patching vulnerabilities was not performed)

### Recommended Next Actions

1. Conduct a thorough review of the incident response plan to identify areas for improvement.
2. Perform a forensic analysis of the attack to determine the root cause and dwell time.
3. Update the IR plan, detection rules, and backups/containment procedures based on lessons learned from this incident.
4. Notify relevant stakeholders and regulatory bodies.
5. Conduct a post-incident review to assess the effectiveness of the response efforts.
